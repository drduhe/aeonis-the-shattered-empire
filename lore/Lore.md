## Worldbuilding and History

The realm of Aeonis is a vast and diverse land, home to myriad cultures, magical nexuses, and ancient ruins. Once united under the banner of the Eternal Empire, the realm has fractured into competing factions, each vying for dominance in the wake of the empire's fall.

## Factions and Lords

Aeonis features a diverse array of factions, each led by a powerful Lord with unique abilities and backstories. From the arcane mastery of the Magi Guild to the martial prowess of the Iron Vanguard, every faction brings its own flavor to the game.

### Example Lord: Kaelthar, The Flamekeeper

Kaelthar is the last surviving scion of the Dragonkin, wielding the power of ancient fire. His fiery temper and unmatched skill in battle make him a force to be reckoned with. As the Flamekeeper, Kaelthar seeks to reignite the ancient dragon forges and restore his people's legacy.

## Significant Locations

Aeonis is dotted with landmarks that hold strategic and narrative importance. From the towering City of Eryndor to the cursed ruins of the Shadowspire, each location offers unique opportunities and dangers.

### The Worldheart

The Worldheart is a mystical nexus of power, said to be the source of all magic in Aeonis. Controlling this location is crucial for any Lord seeking ultimate dominance.

## Stories, Myths, and Legends

Aeonis is rich with tales of heroism and tragedy. From the legend of the Skyflame Wyrm to the mythical forging of the Eversteel Blade, these stories inspire and guide the Lords in their quests for power.

### The Skyflame Wyrm

The Skyflame Wyrm is a legendary dragon said to soar above the highest peaks of Aeonis. Its fiery breath can level armies, and its scales are rumored to be impenetrable. Many Lords have sought to tame the Wyrm, but none have succeeded.
