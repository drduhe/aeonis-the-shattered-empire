# Aeonis: Naming Bible

The terminology and naming canon for **Aeonis: The Shattered Empire**. All content — rules text, cards, Lord sheets, flavor — must use the canonical terms below. When you coin a new proper noun anywhere in the project, register it here.

---

## 1. Defined terms glossary

| Canonical term | Meaning | Forbidden synonyms / notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Aeonis** | The realm; the single landmass on which the game takes place. Named for House Aeon. | — |
| **The Eternal Empire** | The fallen empire of House Aeon that ruled Aeonis for millennia. | Not "the Old Empire" as a formal name (fine as loose prose). |
| **The Shattering** | The cataclysm (~Year 2600) that collapsed the empire, extinguished magic, and destroyed the portal network. | Never "the Fall" as a formal term. |
| **The Age of Lords** | The current era (~Year 2700), a century after the Shattering. | — |
| **The Imperial Seat** | The colossal city at the world's center holding the empty throne. Controlling it confers legitimacy. | **Never "Throne of Power".** "The throne" (lowercase, prose) refers to the seat inside it. |
| **Speaking Stones** | Enchanted obsidian fragments carved by imperial artisans; each Lord carries one. Source of Whispers. | **Never "Palantír".** |
| **Whispers / Whisper Cards** | Intelligence and secret preparations delivered through the Speaking Stones (the shared tactical card deck; see `Whispers.md`). | — |
| **Influence** | The political resource. | **Never "IP".** |
| **Renown** | Reputation/legitimacy track (see `Renown.md`). | Not "Fame", not "Prestige". |
| **Remnants** | Fragments of imperial workmanship gathered from ruins; 3 may be purged to draw an Artifact (see `Artifacts.md`). | — |
| **Artifacts** | Named, unique relics of the Eternal Empire (24-card Artifact Deck). | Not "relics" as a formal term (except the "Building Relic" category). |
| **Discovery / Ritual** | The units of magic in rules terms (see `Arcane.md`). | **Never "spell" as a rules term.** There is no "Cast Spells" action. |
| **The World Tree** / **World Tree King** | The living source of all magic in Aeonis; the deity of its foremost faith. "World Tree King" is the deity's formal name. | — |
| **The High Council** | The surviving imperial political body; convenes at the Imperial Seat (see `High_Council.md`). | — |
| **The Banished Lords** | The eight (and counting) contenders for the throne; exiles, outcasts, and heirs of the empire's betrayed allies. | — |
| **The Great Gate** | The last Emperor's final portal, whose opening triggered the Shattering. | — |
| **Sap Wells** | The imperial bore-shafts sunk into the World Tree's roots during the Waning. | — |
| **The Cult of the Undying Emperor** | Rival creed to the Tree-faith: holds the last Emperor crossed over and will return. | — |
| **Portals / the portal network** | The empire's gate network; surviving Portal hexes on the map (see `Tiles.md`, `Movement.md`). | — |

### Retired / forbidden faction names

The placeholder names **Magi Guild**, **Iron Vanguard**, and **Sacred Order** are not factions in Aeonis and must not appear in any content. **Kaelthar, the Flamekeeper** is canon only as a legendary historical figure / rumored returning power (see `Lore.md`, Legends & Mysteries) — he is **not** one of the eight playable Lords.

### Race definitions (one-line canon)

- **Human** — the empire's majority people; three distinct cultures survive among the Lords (Amber Reach mercantile, Ironveil martial, Leywilds arcane).
- **Elven** — the realm's eldest people, bound to the forests by the Covenant, betrayed by the empire's expansion.
- **Djinnborn** — descendants of imperial outriders who bound wind spirits into their bloodlines in the Age of Wonders.
- **Umbral** — shadow-touched humanoids born from imperial dimensional-phasing experiments in the empire's twilight years.
- **Luminari** — crystalline beings formed from the World Tree's sap during the Shattering, carrying fragments of divine memory.
- **Voidborn** — beings of shimmering static that coalesced from the collapsed portal network's pooled dimensional energy.
- **Tideborn** *(expansion)* — the changed descendants of the coastal provinces the empire drowned; grey-eyed, salt-blooded, water-breathing.
- **Revenant** *(expansion)* — the risen dead of the Shattering's broken boundary; soldiers who kept marching after death.
- **Ashkarr** *(expansion)* — the horn-crowned wagon-people of the Long Steppe who drove the empire's supply trains; the Kessarin are their nine clans.

## 4. Naming conventions per culture

Patterns are derived from the established names; example names are pre-approved for future content (units, characters, cards, places).

### Amber Reach humans (Golden Consortium — cf. Cassian)

Latinate and mercantile: soft consonants, two to three syllables, endings in *-an, -io, -a, -or*. Names sound like signatures on contracts.
**Examples:** Lucrezio, Petrona, Valcor, Marcetta, Odovan, Silvane.

### Ironveil humans (Iron Dominion — cf. Vharok)

Hard, percussive, forge-struck: clustered consonants (*kh, rg, dr, br*), one or two heavy syllables. Names sound like hammer blows.
**Examples:** Dhurgan, Morvek, Bregda, Khazrun, Ulmar, Vresk.

### Leywilds humans (Arcanum Concord — cf. Seraphel)

Melodic and scholarly: liquid consonants (*l, r, v, th*), three syllables, endings in *-el, -ane, -in, -elle*. Names sound like incantations half-finished.
**Examples:** Ilvane, Caristhel, Maelrin, Orivelle, Thessane, Auvred.

### Elven (Verdant Covenant — cf. Elyndra)

Flowing and rooted: *y* as a vowel, *-nd-/-lv-/-thr-* clusters, endings in *-ra, -wyn, -del, -ys*. Names evoke growth and water.
**Examples:** Caelwyn, Ilyndor, Sylthara, Verandel, Wrenlyss, Thalyndra.

### Djinnborn (Windsworn Horde — cf. Rakhis)

Desert-wind names: emphatic *kh/q/z* sounds, open *a* vowels, one strong stressed syllable. Names sound shouted across dunes.
**Examples:** Sahriq, Zerath, Akhira, Nazhim, Qattara, Rhazan.

### Umbral (Silent Court — cf. Nyxara)

Nocturnal and sibilant: *x, y, v, ss* sounds, muted vowels, endings in *-is, -yx, -yr, -eth*. Names sound whispered.
**Examples:** Vesmyr, Xalith, Nyrissa, Oskyre, Thessyx, Umbrelle.

### Luminari (Radiant Accord — cf. Auriel)

Radiant and liturgical: bright vowel pairs (*au, ia, ie*), soft consonants, endings in *-el, -iel, -essa*. Names sound sung.
**Examples:** Solriel, Verathiel, Lumessa, Auphene, Miradiel, Cantorel.

### Voidborn (The Threshold — cf. Thal'rik)

Fractured, static-torn names: an apostrophe marking a glottal stop between two hard fragments. Names sound like transmissions cutting out.
**Examples:** Vel'shar, Qor'ath, Nym'kel, Ish'varra, Dral'un, Kesh'ta.
