# Aeonis: Positioning & Product Decisions

**Status:** Locked (2026-07-02). These are product-level canon. If a doc contradicts this page, update that doc.

---

## Positioning statement

**"If you love Twilight Imperium, you will probably love Aeonis."**

Aeonis is not competing with TI4 — it is offering TI4's audience an entirely new experience. The differentiation is the **setting and everything it unlocks**:

- TI4 is sci-fi; Aeonis is **epic fantasy**. Same grandeur, new world.
- The fantasy backdrop opens mechanical space a space opera can't reach: **Lords as capturable heroes on the map**, land warfare with **sieges, flanking cavalry, and zones of control**, **Arcane Discovery** in place of technology, the **Whisper** intelligence deck, **Artifacts and Remnants** unearthed from a fallen empire, and a **High Council** where players author their own motions.
- The pitch is additive, not adversarial: Aeonis is the game a TI4 group plays when they want the same epic weight in a world of steel and sorcery.

### Approved language

- "In the tradition of the great epic 4X board games."
- "Inspired by the genre TI4 defined." / "Inspired by epic space operas — reforged in fantasy."
- Direct, friendly comparisons ("for fans of Twilight Imperium") are fine in community/press contexts.
- **Never** describe Aeonis as a TI4 clone, derivative, or "reskin." All terminology, lore, and card text stay original.

## Locked product decisions

### 1. Session length: 4–10 hours

- The complexity and length are part of the allure to the hardcore strategy audience — we target it deliberately, matching the real-world TI4 experience.
- Full game target: **4–10 hours** depending on player count and VP variant (8 VP short game trims the low end).
- The **First Playable packet remains a compressed testbed** (reduced decks, Tier I Arcane only) so playtest iteration stays fast; its shorter session target is a testing convenience, not the product target.

### 2. Player count: 3–8

- Committed: **3–8 players**. The council, trade, and negotiation systems assume a multipolar table.
- A 2-player mode is out of scope for launch (revisit post-launch as a variant or expansion).

### 3. Components: acrylic standees, miniatures as stretch goal

- Base game: **acrylic standees** for all units and Lords; tokens/tiles for everything else.
- **Custom Lord miniatures** are the flagship Kickstarter **stretch goal** (one sculpt per Lord).
- All component design, cost modeling, and MSRP math assume the standee baseline; miniatures are priced as incremental stretch-goal cost, not baked into the funding goal.

### 4. Audience

- Primary: the hardcore epic-strategy tabletop community — TI4, Eclipse, Heroes of Land Air & Sea, Arcs/Oath players; BGG-weight 3.5+ comfort.
- These players already accept long sessions, deep rules, and negotiation-heavy play. Marketing speaks to them first; broader strategy gamers second.
