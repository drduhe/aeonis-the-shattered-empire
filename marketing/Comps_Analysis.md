# Aeonis: Comparable Titles Analysis (Comps)

**Purpose:** ground the Kickstarter's pricing, funding goal, and messaging in what comparable games actually promised, charged, and delivered. Figures are approximate and should be re-verified against live listings ~3 months before campaign launch.

Positioning context: see `Positioning.md` (locked: 4-10 hour sessions, 3-8 players, acrylic standees baseline, Lord miniatures as stretch goal).

---

## 1. The direct comparables

### Twilight Imperium 4th Edition (FFG, 2017, retail — no crowdfunding)
- **What it is:** the genre benchmark; 3-6p (8 with PoK), 4-8+ hours, ~$150 MSRP core + ~$100 expansion.
- **Lesson for Aeonis:** the audience at this weight exists and pays premium prices, but they expect enormous production value and total rules confidence. We cannot out-produce FFG; we can out-theme and out-innovate (capturable Lords, Whispers, land warfare).

### Heroes of Land, Air & Sea (Gamelyn, Kickstarter 2017)
- **What it is:** the closest "fantasy 4X epic on Kickstarter" comp; raised ~$700K+ across campaigns; heavy miniatures strategy.
- **Lesson:** fantasy 4X funds well on KS; miniatures drove the pledge average but also the delays and freight pain. Supports our standees-first decision (D3).

### Eclipse: Second Dawn for the Galaxy (Lautapelit, Kickstarter 2018)
- **What it is:** ~€2.5M raise; premium reprint of a 4X classic; ~$120-250 tiers.
- **Lesson:** deluxe component storytelling (trays, inserts) moved pledges as much as gameplay. Budget for an insert story even with standees.

### Arcs (Leder, Kickstarter 2022) & Oath (Leder, 2020)
- **What they are:** ~$1.4M and ~$2.4M raises; medium-heavy space/fantasy politics; $60-110 tiers; strong art identity, restrained components.
- **Lesson:** a distinctive art direction + a designer voice can outperform miniature spam. A coherent visual identity is our highest-leverage art spend.

### Tidal Blades 2 / Stormsunder / other "epic fantasy KS" (misc.)
- **Lesson (cautionary):** oversized campaigns with sprawling content promises suffered multi-year delays and refund storms. Scope containment (core box + defined stretch ladder) is a trust feature; say so on the page.

## 2. What the comps charged (pricing bands)

| Band | Examples | Contents pattern |
| --- | --- | --- |
| $59-79 | Arcs core | restrained components, cards + tokens |
| $99-129 | Oath deluxe, HLAS base | premium bits, some minis or deluxe tokens |
| $139-179 | TI4 retail, Eclipse 2nd Dawn KS mid-tier | full flagship production |
| $200+ | all-in tiers | expansions + deluxe upgrades bundled |

**Aeonis draft targets (standee baseline, to be validated by manufacturer quotes in Phase 5):**
- **Core pledge:** $99-119 (8 Lords, full decks, standees, hex map)
- **Deluxe/all-in:** $149-179 (Lord miniatures stretch content, metal/deluxe tokens, playmat or neoprene if costed)
- **Retail MSRP after KS:** core ~$120-130

## 3. Funding-goal logic (fill in with quotes)

```
Funding goal = (manufacturing at MOQ ~1,500 units)
             + (freight to 3PLs, est. 15-20% of manufacturing)
             + (art & graphic design remaining budget)
             + (platform + payment fees ~10% of raise)
             + (15% contingency)
```

Rule of thumb from comps: set the public goal at the **minimum viable print run**, not the hoped-for raise — every comp above funded low-goal/overshoot campaigns; high goals stall momentum.

## 4. Messaging takeaways for the campaign page

1. Lead with the fantasy: "the epic council-table war game where your Lord rides at the front of the army — and can be taken prisoner."
2. Comparison framing per `Positioning.md` (D2/D5): "If you love Twilight Imperium, you will probably love Aeonis" — additive, never "TI4 killer."
3. Show, early on the page: a full table shot, one battle diagram, one council vote moment, one Lord sheet.
4. Trust signals: logged playtest count, blind-test quotes, a locked component list, a realistic timeline with buffer.
5. Stretch ladder from already-designed content only (Lord minis first, then Lords 9-12 — sheets already exist, then advanced units).
