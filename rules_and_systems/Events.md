# Aeonis: Events

## Overview

The Event System introduces dynamic, unpredictable elements that enhance replayability and immerse players in the evolving story of the game. Events are triggered through exploration, High Council decisions, milestones, or game-wide effects. They create opportunities and challenges, often forcing impactful decisions that shape the game state.

---

## Event Types

### 1. Exploration Events

- **Trigger:** When a player enters an unexplored hex during the Action Phase.
- **Timing:** Resolve **immediately upon entry** (see `Round_Structure.md`, section 5.3).
- **Outcome:** Each hex has a corresponding event card with results such as discovering resources, encountering NPCs, triggering traps, or finding ruins.
- **Deck:** the full 24-card deck is listed in the **Full Deck Lists** section at the end of this chapter (First Playable uses a 9-card subset).

### 2. Global Events

- **Trigger:** Drawn and resolved during the **Event Phase** (see `Round_Structure.md`, section 2).
- **Effect:** Impact all players, creating realm-wide challenges or opportunities. Examples: natural disasters, increased resource production, or temporary rule changes.
- **Deck:** the full 24-card deck is listed in the **Full Deck Lists** section at the end of this chapter (First Playable uses a 12-card subset).

### 3. Localized Events

- **Trigger:** Impact specific hexes, factions, or resources.
- **Effect:** Examples include rebellions, magical corruption, or hex-specific penalties/bonuses.

### 4. Milestone Events

- **Trigger:** Reaching specific game milestones, such as earning 10 Renown, building a Legendary structure, or controlling a key number of hexes.
- **Effect:** Significant, story-driven events with game-changing consequences.
- **Canon list:** the six full-game milestones are defined in the **Full Deck Lists** section at the end of this chapter.

---

## Event Triggers

1. **Exploration:** Drawing an Exploration Event card reveals the hex's contents. Outcomes may include rewards, obstacles, or both.

2. **High Council Decisions:** Passing certain motions can trigger events, such as realm-wide decrees or localized effects.

3. **Event Phase (each round):** A Global Event card is drawn at the start of the Event Phase and resolved immediately.

4. **Artifacts and Rituals:** Specific artifacts or Rituals trigger localized or game-wide events.

5. **Milestones:** Reaching significant game achievements unleashes powerful, thematic events.

---

## Event Outcomes

### Rewards

- Players gain resources, Renown, or artifacts through beneficial event results.

### Challenges

- Some events introduce obstacles, such as:
  - Hex penalties (e.g., reduced resource production).
  - Immediate costs (e.g., losing units or resources).
  - Defending against invaders or quelling rebellions.

### Choices

- Many events require players to make strategic decisions:
  - Aid a settlement for Renown at a resource cost.
  - Exploit a magical anomaly for resources but risk long-term penalties.

### Persistent Effects

- Certain events permanently affect hexes or the game state (e.g., a hex becomes corrupted and produces no resources).

---

## Catch-Up Events (canon)

These events specifically help trailing players stay competitive. They are mixed into the global event deck and create moments where falling behind is not a death spiral.

### Populist Uprising

- **Type:** Global Event
- **Effect:** The player(s) with the **fewest controlled hexes** gain **+2 Population Pool** (up to cap) and **+1 Influence**. If tied for fewest, all tied players gain the bonus.

### Winds of Fortune

- **Type:** Global Event
- **Effect:** The player(s) with the **fewest VP** gain **+2 AP** next round (added during Round Start) and may **draw one additional objective card** (public or secret, their choice; if the objective deck is empty, gain +1 Renown instead). If tied for fewest VP, all tied players gain the bonus.

A third catch-up event, **The Realm Remembers**, is defined in the Full Deck Lists below.

## Synergy with Other Systems

### 1. High Council Influence

- Some events may only occur if a motion is passed, reinforcing the political layer. Example: Passing a "Realm Tax" motion leads to increased Gold but reduces Influence.

### 2. Exploration and Risk/Reward

- Hex exploration becomes a calculated risk, as events can offer rewards (e.g., resources, artifacts) or penalties (e.g., traps, invaders).

### 3. Renown Synergy

- Certain events grant or deduct Renown based on the player's choices, reinforcing the fame/reputation mechanic.

### 4. Action Points (AP) Impact

- Events may modify AP directly:
  - Example: "Unrest: Lose 1 AP unless you spend 2 Influence."
  - Example: "Festival: Gain +1 AP this round."

### 5. Population System

- Events like famine or rebellion tie directly into the Population System, introducing penalties or opportunities to grow.

### 6. Artifact System

- Events are the primary way Artifact Sites appear on the map. Global and Exploration events that create Artifact Sites drive territorial conflict around valuable relic locations. See `Artifacts.md`.

## Example Events (full card text samples)

These entries show the long-form card text for a few events. The canonical deck contents are in the **Full Deck Lists** section below; where a deck entry is a one-line summary, the long-form text here governs.

### Exploration Event: Ancient Ruins

- **Description:** You stumble upon the remains of a forgotten temple.
- **Options:**
    1. Search the ruins. Roll a die: On a 4+, gain 2 Remnants (see `Artifacts.md`). On 3 or less, lose 1 unit to a trap.
    2. Leave the ruins untouched. Gain +1 Renown for your caution.

### Global Event: Harsh Winter

- **Description:** A brutal winter strikes the realm.
- **Effect:** Each player loses 2 Gold unless they control at least 1 Farm.

### Localized Event: Rebellion

- **Description:** The population of a hex revolts against your rule.
- **Effect:** The hex produces no resources until 2 Influence is spent to quell the unrest.

### Global Event: Ruins Unearthed

- **Description:** An earthquake reveals the entrance to an ancient imperial vault.
- **Effect:** Place an **Artifact Site marker** on a neutral or uncontrolled Ruins hex (if none available, place on any neutral hex). Draw the top Artifact Card and place it face-up on the marker. Any player with a unit there may spend 1 AP to claim it (see `Artifacts.md`).

### Global Event: Echo of the Old Empire

- **Description:** A Speaking Stone resonates with a frequency not heard in a hundred years, guiding seekers to buried treasure.
- **Effect:** Place an **Artifact Site marker** on the hex closest to the Imperial Seat that does not already have one (break ties clockwise from Speaker). Draw the top Artifact Card and place it face-up on the marker (see `Artifacts.md`). All players gain 1 Remnant.

### Exploration Event: Ancient Vault Discovered

- **Description:** Behind a collapsed wall, you find a sealed chamber marked with imperial insignia.
- **Effect:** Place an **Artifact Site marker** on this hex. Draw the top Artifact Card and place it face-up here. You may immediately spend 1 AP to claim it (see `Artifacts.md`). If you do not, it remains for any player to claim.

### Exploration Event: Scattered Relics

- **Description:** The remains of an ancient artifact litter the ground around a shattered monument.
- **Effect:** Gain 2 Remnants (see `Artifacts.md`).

## Full Deck Lists (Full Game)

### Event VP budget (Plan 3 D5)

Event-driven VP stays in the design (dramatic moments), but:

- **No single event awards more than 1 VP** to one player.
- Across both full decks, expect **at most ~2 VP** from events to one player per game in typical play.
- Prefer tempo, Remnants, Renown, and Influence over raw VP on event cards.

### Deck usage

- **First Playable:** use only the cards marked **(FP)** below — 12 Global Events and 9 Exploration Events. The printing list for playtests stays in `../playtest/First_Playable_Packet.md`, section 4.5; that packet remains the reference for what is "on" in playtests.
- **Full game:** shuffle all **24 Global Events** into the global event deck and all **24 Exploration Events** into the exploration event deck. During setup, shuffle **The Shattering Storm** into the **bottom half** of the global deck so the crisis lands in the mid/late game.
- **Milestone events** are a full-game module (6 events, defined below). They may be added to First Playable, except where noted (**The Deep Arts Return** requires Tier III research, which First Playable disables).

### Global Event Deck (24 cards)

**Timing (all cards):** drawn and resolved during the **Event Phase**, before Strategy Selection (see `Round_Structure.md`, section 2). Effects last until end of the current round unless a **Duration** line says otherwise.

#### First Playable globals (12) — verbatim from the packet (FP)

- **Harsh Winter**: Each player loses 2 Gold unless they control at least 1 Farm.
- **Festival**: All players gain +1 AP next round.
- **Migration Wave**: Each player with at least 2 Plains gains +2 Population Pool (up to cap).
- **Council Crisis**: The Speaker must put a motion on the agenda; if it fails, Speaker loses 1 Renown.
- **Mana Surge**: Each player gains +2 Mana.
- **Border Skirmishes**: The player with the most controlled hexes gains +1 Renown; ties: all tied gain +1 Renown.
- **Supply Disruption**: Each player must discard 2 total resources (any mix of Gold/Mana/Influence).
- **Open Roads**: Until end of round, movement across Plains costs 1 less AP (min 1).
- **Populist Uprising** (catch-up): The player(s) with the fewest controlled hexes gain +2 Population Pool (up to cap) and +1 Influence.
- **Winds of Fortune** (catch-up): The player(s) with the fewest VP gain +2 AP next round and may draw one additional objective card (public or secret, their choice).
- **Ruins Unearthed**: Place an Artifact Site marker on a neutral/uncontrolled Ruins hex (or any neutral hex). Draw the top Artifact Card face-up on it (see `Artifacts.md`).
- **Echo of the Old Empire**: Place an Artifact Site marker on the hex closest to the Imperial Seat without one. Draw the top Artifact Card face-up. All players gain 1 Remnant (see `Artifacts.md`).

(Long-form canon text for Populist Uprising and Winds of Fortune lives in the **Catch-Up Events** section above; for Ruins Unearthed and Echo of the Old Empire, in **Example Events**.)

#### Additional globals (12) — full game only

- **The Realm Remembers** (catch-up): The player(s) with the **fewest VP** gain **+1 Renown** and **+2 Influence**. If tied for fewest VP, all tied players gain the bonus. *(Third catch-up card; Renown + Influence feeds council votes rather than raw tempo, so the three catch-up cards help in different ways.)*
- **Golden Caravans**: Each player gains **1 Gold per City** they control (maximum 3 Gold). *(Capped so wide empires don't turn an economy swing into a runaway.)*
- **Muster Call**: Until end of round, a **Recruit** action may recruit up to **3 units per City** (instead of 2; see `Actions.md`). All other Recruit costs and limits apply.
- **Mountain Passes Cleared**: Until end of round, entering a **Mountain** hex costs **1 AP** (instead of 2; see `Movement.md`).
- **Plague**: Each player must remove **2 available Population Points** from their Population Pool (points tied up by units and buildings in play are unaffected; a player with fewer than 2 available loses what they have). A player who controls at least **2 Farms** is unaffected (see `Population.md`). *(Farm mitigation mirrors Harsh Winter: infrastructure is the insurance policy.)*
- **Relic Fever**: Each player gains **1 Remnant**. Until end of round, purging Remnants to draw an Artifact Card costs **2 Remnants** (instead of 3; see `Artifacts.md`).
- **Whispers of the Deep Vaults**: Each player with a unit **on or adjacent to a Ruins hex** gains **2 Remnants**; every other player gains **1 Remnant** (see `Artifacts.md`). *(Rewards map position without leaving anyone empty-handed.)*
- **Emergency Session**: During this round's High Council Phase, proposing a motion costs **0 Influence** (instead of 1), and the Speaker reveals **two** agenda deck cards instead of one (each may be proposed for free as normal; see `High_Council.md`, §3.2a).
- **Purse Strings of Power**: During this round's High Council Phase, lobbying costs **1 Influence per vote** (instead of 2 Influence per vote; see `High_Council.md`, §2.3).
- **The Long Drought** (persistent): During Production & Upkeep **this round and the next round**, each **Plains** hex produces 1 less Population (minimum 0). **Duration:** 2 rounds. **Expiry:** discard this card at the second Cleanup & Checks after it resolves. *(Two rounds is long enough to force a plan change, short enough not to bury Plains strategies.)*
- **Ley Line Flux** (persistent): **This round and the next round**: each **Forest** hex produces +1 Mana during Production & Upkeep, and entering or exiting a **Portal** hex costs **0 AP** (instead of 1; Portal-to-Portal travel is unchanged, see `Movement.md`). **Duration:** 2 rounds. **Expiry:** discard this card at the second Cleanup & Checks after it resolves.
- **The Shattering Storm** (crisis, persistent): The wound that broke the empire tears open above the old capital.
  - Place a **Storm marker** on the **Imperial Seat** hex and on each hex adjacent to it.
  - While a hex has a Storm marker: it produces **nothing** during Production & Upkeep; entering it costs **+1 AP**; and the Imperial Seat's **Coronation Rite** (see `Victory.md`) **cannot be scored** while its marker remains.
  - **Cleansing:** On your turn in the Action Phase, if you have a unit on a Storm hex, you may spend **2 AP** to remove that hex's Storm marker (this is your action for the turn). You gain **+1 Renown** for each marker you remove.
  - **Duration:** persistent. **Expiry:** at the second Cleanup & Checks after this event resolves, remove all remaining Storm markers and discard this card.
  - **Setup note:** shuffle this card into the bottom half of the global deck. *(A shared threat with no NPC units: the storm punishes whoever holds the center — usually the leader — and pays Renown to whoever does the cleanup work.)*

### Exploration Event Deck (24 cards)

**Timing (all cards):** resolve **immediately upon first entry** to an unexplored hex during the Action Phase (see `Round_Structure.md`, section 5.3). "You" is the entering player; "the entering group" is the unit group that triggered the event.

#### First Playable exploration events (9) — verbatim from the packet (FP)

- **Ancient Ruins**: Search the ruins — roll a die: on 4+, gain 2 Remnants (see `Artifacts.md`); on 3 or less, lose 1 unit to a trap. Or leave untouched for +1 Renown.
- **Trapped Vault**: Lose 1 unit from the entering group OR pay 2 Gold to avoid.
- **Speaking Stone Echo**: Gain +1 Influence and +1 Renown.
- **Lost Cartographer**: Reveal any one unrevealed tile (if using fog) and gain +1 AP this round.
- **Wandering Mercenaries**: Gain 1 Infantry for free (place in the entering hex if legal; otherwise your nearest controlled City).
- **Cursed Ground**: The hex produces no resources until you spend 2 Influence to cleanse it.
- **Scattered Relics**: Gain 2 Remnants (see `Artifacts.md`).
- **Portal Instability**: If the hex is a Portal, you may immediately Portal travel once at 0 AP (destination must be neutral or yours).
- **Ancient Vault Discovered**: Place an Artifact Site marker on this hex. Draw the top Artifact Card face-up. You may spend 1 AP to claim it immediately (see `Artifacts.md`).

#### Additional exploration events (15) — full game only

Mix: 8 straight rewards, 3 strategic choices, 3 risky choices, 1 pure risk — most draws feel good, a few sting (TI4 exploration pacing).

- **Fertile Valley** (reward): Gain **+1 Population Pool** (up to cap). If this hex is **Plains**, gain **+2** instead (see `Population.md`).
- **Gold Vein** (reward): Gain **1 Gold**. If this hex is a **Mountain**, gain **3 Gold** instead.
- **Mana Bloom** (reward): Gain **2 Mana**. If this hex is a **Forest**, gain **3 Mana** instead.
- **Imperial Tax Cache** (reward): Gain **2 Gold** and **1 Influence**.
- **Overheard Whisper** (reward): Draw **1 Whisper Card** (see `Whispers.md`).
- **Speaking Stone Fragment** (reward): Gain **+1 Influence**. You may look at the top card of the agenda deck (see `High_Council.md`, §3.2a).
- **Hidden Waystone** (reward): The entering group may immediately move **1 additional hex at 0 AP** (normal movement restrictions and ZOC surcharges apply; see `Movement.md`).
- **Old Battlefield** (reward + choice): Gain **1 Remnant** (see `Artifacts.md`). You may pay **1 Gold** to bury the dead with honor: gain **+1 Renown**.
- **Abandoned Shrine** (choice): Choose one — **Kneel**: gain **+1 Renown**; or **Loot the altar**: gain **2 Gold** and lose **1 Renown** (minimum 0).
- **Refugee Column** (choice): Choose one — **Shelter them**: pay **1 Gold** to gain **+2 Population Pool** (up to cap) and **+1 Renown**; or **Turn them away**: no effect.
- **Deserters' Camp** (choice): Choose one — **Press them into service**: gain **1 Infantry** in this hex, paying its Population cost as normal (you may not choose this if you cannot pay), and lose **1 Renown** (minimum 0); or **Grant amnesty**: gain **+1 Renown**.
- **Collapsed Mine** (risky choice): You may send diggers in — roll a die: on **4+**, gain **3 Gold**; on **3 or less**, lose 1 unit from the entering group. Or leave it sealed: no effect.
- **Sealed Reliquary** (risky choice): Choose one — pay **2 Mana** to open it safely: gain **2 Remnants**; or force it open — roll a die: on **4+**, gain **2 Remnants**; on **3 or less**, lose 1 unit from the entering group. Or leave it: no effect.
- **Forgotten Watchtower** (risky choice): You may pay **2 Gold** and **1 Population** to restore it: place a **Tower** on this hex at no AP cost (see `Buildings.md`). If you do not (or cannot), gain **1 Gold** salvaged from the rubble.
- **Unstable Ground** (risk): Roll a die: on **1–2**, lose 1 unit from the entering group (if the group is only your Lord, your Lord takes 1 damage instead; see `Combat.md`, §2.1); on **3+**, no effect.

### Milestone Events (6)

Milestone events are table-wide, story-driven moments. Rules:

- Each milestone triggers **at most once per game**, the first time any player meets its trigger condition.
- **Timing:** when a trigger condition is met, mark that milestone. The milestone event resolves at the next **Milestone Check** step of the Event Phase (see `Round_Structure.md`, section 2, step 3). If more than one milestone is pending, resolve them in the order they were triggered.
- Milestone effects are deliberately **table-wide** — the triggering player already collected the underlying reward (VP, Renown, the building, the capture); the milestone marks the moment for everyone.

1. **Foundations of a New Age**
   - **Trigger:** the first **Legendary Building** is constructed by any player (see `Buildings.md`).
   - **Event:** Dormant ley lines reawaken around the new wonder. Each player gains **+2 Mana**. Until end of round, each player's first **Research** action costs **1 less Mana** (minimum 0; see `Arcane.md`).

2. **First Blood of Lords**
   - **Trigger:** a **Lord is captured** for the first time (see `Combat.md`, §2.1.4).
   - **Event:** The realm recoils — no Lord has been taken since the empire fell. Each player gains **+1 Influence**. During this round's High Council Phase, proposing a motion costs **0 Influence** (the council demands answers).

3. **Hero of the Age**
   - **Trigger:** a player reaches **10 Renown** for the first time (see `Renown.md`).
   - **Event:** Festivals erupt across the realm in the hero's name. Every player gains **+1 AP this round**.

4. **The Empty Throne Stirs**
   - **Trigger:** a player gains control of the **Imperial Seat** for the first time (by any method; see `Tiles.md` for control rules).
   - **Event:** The Speaking Stones cry out across Aeonis. Each player gains **1 Remnant** and **+1 Influence**. Then place an **Artifact Site marker** on the neutral hex closest to the Imperial Seat that does not already have one (break ties clockwise from Speaker; if no neutral hex is available, skip this step) and place the top Artifact Card face-up on it (see `Artifacts.md`).

5. **The Deep Arts Return** *(full game only — First Playable restricts research to Tier I)*
   - **Trigger:** the first **Tier III Arcane Discovery** is researched by any player (see `Arcane.md`).
   - **Event:** Raw magic floods back into the world. Each player gains **+2 Mana**. Each player who controls an **Academy** also gains **1 Remnant**.

6. **The Final Contest**
   - **Trigger:** a player reaches **8 VP** for the first time (see `Victory.md`).
   - **Event:** The realm braces for a coronation. Each player with **fewer than 8 VP** gains **+1 Influence** and **+1 AP this round** (banners rally against a would-be emperor).
