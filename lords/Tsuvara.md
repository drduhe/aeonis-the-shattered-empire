# Lord Sheet: Tsuvara, Mother of Wagons

**Status:** Expansion roster — not yet in the First Playable rotation.

## Lord Overview

**Name:** Tsuvara, Mother of Wagons
**Race:** Ashkarr
**Faction Theme:** The Endless Caravan
**Gameplay Style:** Nomadic migration, a mobile home City, terrain adaptability, and treaty-driven prosperity — an economy that walks away from threats and toward opportunity.

## Lore

> "Land is not something you own. It is something you are passing through."

The Ashkarr never had a province to lose when the empire fell — they were its roads. Horn-crowned and weather-worn, they drove the great supply trains that fed the imperial legions, and when the legions dissolved, the Ashkarr simply kept driving. Their wagons grew into rolling halls, their halls into a rolling city, and somewhere in a hard winter three generations ago, the Endless Caravan stopped being a convoy and became a nation.

Tsuvara has led the Caravan for forty years, and in that time it has never camped twice in the same place. She has outrolled famines, outwaited sieges that found only wheel-ruts, and signed more treaties than the rest of the realm combined — because everyone, eventually, needs what the Caravan carries. Rakhis raids and vanishes; Tsuvara arrives and stays exactly as long as it profits her. Then the horns sound, the wheels turn, and the city is somewhere else.

### Unique Starting Tile (replaces your City — this IS her Home City)

**The Great Caravan** (counts as **City**)

- **Production:** +2 Population and +1 Gold
- **Building permissions:** As a City (up to 2 buildings; 3 with research). Buildings constructed here are wagon-borne and **travel with the Caravan** when it moves (see Wheels of the Ashkarr below). You may **not** build a **Fortress** or a **Castle** here (nothing about the Caravan is permanent).

- *Capture note: The Great Caravan follows standard control rules (see `Tiles.md`) — it can be besieged and captured like any City. A captor gains the City and its buildings but cannot move it (Wheels of the Ashkarr is Tsuvara's faction mechanic, not a tile property).*

## Abilities

### 1) Wheels of the Ashkarr (Faction Mechanic)

**Effect:** Once per round, on your turn during the **Action Phase**, you may pay **1 AP** to move the **Great Caravan** (the City tile itself, with all your buildings on it) **1 hex**. Your units in the hex move with it at no additional cost.
**Restrictions:**

- The destination must be a hex you **control** or a **neutral** hex containing no other player's units (moving there claims it, per `Tiles.md`).
- The Caravan cannot enter a **Lake**, a **Portal**, another **City** hex (including the Imperial Seat), or any hex in an enemy **ZOC** (see `Tiles.md`).
- The hex the Caravan leaves reverts to its underlying terrain (place the terrain tile it was covering); you retain control of it per normal control rules.
- The Caravan cannot move while it is under **Siege** (see `Combat.md` §6).

**Rules reference:** `Tiles.md` (control, ZOC), `Combat.md` (sieges), `Movement.md`.
**Theme:** The city was never the point. The wheels were.

### 2) Children of Every Road (Passive Ability)

**Effect:** Your units treat **Mountains and Deserts** as costing **2 AP** but ignore any **non-AP terrain drawback** of hexes they occupy (e.g., the Desert rule "units lose 1 Population if they spend more than 2 turns here," see `Movement.md`). In addition, while an **Open Borders Treaty** or any treaty motion naming you is in effect (see `High_Council.md`), gain **+1 Gold** during **Production & Upkeep**.
**Theme:** The Caravan has wintered in every terrain Aeonis has — and it pays to be welcome.

### 3) The Long Halt (Active Ability)

**Effect:** Once per round during the Action Phase, on your turn (costs **0 AP**, but consumes your turn like an ACTION), if the Great Caravan did **not** move this round, gain **+1 Population Point** (up to your Population Cap) and **+1 Gold**.
**Cost:** Forgoing the Caravan's movement this round
**Theme:** When the wheels rest, the market opens and the children are born.

## Faction Research (The Endless Caravan)

### Outrider Trails

**School:** TRN
**Tier:** I (1 AP to research)
**Prerequisite:** none
**Cost:** 1 Mana, 1 Gold (Research action)
**Type:** Passive
**Effect:** Once per round, when you take a **Move** action with a group that starts in or adjacent to the **Great Caravan**, reduce the total AP cost of that move by **1** (minimum 1).
**Theme:** The Caravan's outriders know every shortcut worth knowing.

### Rolling Bazaar

**School:** TRN
**Tier:** II (2 AP to research)
**Prerequisite:** TRN 1
**Cost:** 2 Gold, 2 Influence (Research action)
**Type:** Passive
**Effect:** The Great Caravan counts as containing a **Market** (once per round, initiate one Trade at 0 AP; see `Buildings.md`) without occupying a building slot. In addition, once per round, when another player initiates a Trade **with you**, gain **+1 Influence**.
**Theme:** Wherever the Caravan halts, a marketplace blooms by nightfall.

## Faction Objectives

### Primary Objective (2 VP): "The Long Road Home"

The **Great Caravan** has moved in **4 different rounds**, and you control the hex it currently occupies plus **2 or more hexes adjacent** to it at **Cleanup & Checks**.

### Secondary Objective (1 VP): "Everyone Needs the Caravan"

At **Cleanup & Checks**, a treaty motion naming you (e.g., **Open Borders Treaty**, see `High_Council.md`) is in effect, and you completed at least **1 Trade** this round.

## Faction Strategy

### Strengths

- **An economy that dodges:** The Caravan simply leaves before a war arrives. Sieges against Tsuvara hit air; her whole production core repositions toward fresh neutral land each round.
- **Terrain indifference:** Children of Every Road plus the Caravan's terrain-matching production means no part of the map is a bad draw for her.
- **Treaty magnet:** Rolling Bazaar and her treaty bonuses make her the player everyone profits from tolerating — political cover without Auriel's Renown engine.
- **Recruiting on the frontier:** Because Recruit places units at Cities (see `Actions.md`) and her City moves, Tsuvara reinforces frontiers no one else can reinforce.

### Weaknesses

- **One-hex tempo:** 1 hex per round is slow. A committed aggressor can herd the Caravan into a corner, and ZOC (unit presence) blocks its escape routes entirely.
- **Everything in one wagon:** Her buildings travel with the Caravan — which means capturing the Caravan captures her entire infrastructure in one siege. No Fortress/Castle allowed makes that siege easier than against any other Home City.
- **Soft military:** d6-Attack Lord, standard army, no combat bonuses at all. Tsuvara wins by not fighting.
- **Trailing territory:** Land the Caravan leaves behind is undefended and invites incursion (see `Tiles.md`); her borders are always crumbling at the rear.

## Notes for Playtesting

- **Wheels of the Ashkarr (biggest risk):** A mobile City breaks the map's siege geometry — verify that a determined attacker can actually pin the Caravan (ZOC exclusion is the intended leash; confirm it's enough) and that "recruit anywhere the Caravan rolls" doesn't out-tempo every land war.
- **Caravan capture stakes:** Losing the Caravan loses Tsuvara everything at once. Confirm this is a dramatic but survivable setback (she keeps controlled hexes and units) and not an instant elimination — and conversely that the no-Fortress rule doesn't make rushing her Round 2 a dominant strategy.
- **The Long Halt:** +1 Population +1 Gold for standing still, every round, compounds. Confirm the opportunity cost (no migration, spends her turn) keeps it honest.
- **Terrain-matching production:** Caravan parked on a Mountain effectively produces +2 Gold +2 Population as a City. Check whether any single terrain parking spot is strictly dominant.
