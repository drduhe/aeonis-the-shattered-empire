# Lord Sheet: Seraphel, Lord of the Arcane

## Lord Overview

**Name:** Seraphel, Lord of the Arcane  
**Faction Theme:** The Arcanum Concord  
**Gameplay Style:** Mana advantage, information control, and flexible tempo through efficient combat pacing.

## Lore

> “The battlefield is only a diagram. I prefer to win with the first stroke of chalk.”

Seraphel’s court is a lattice of wards, lenses, and quiet bargains. Where other Lords contest the map with force, Seraphel contests it with **timing**—seeing the agenda before it opens, choosing where battles happen, and turning a single Attack action into a decisive cascade. In the Arcanum Concord, knowledge is not power; it is permission.

## Abilities

### 1) Polymath (Faction Mechanic)

**Timing:** When checking a Discovery's prerequisites, and once per round after you resolve a Research action on your turn in the **Action Phase**.

**Effect:** Count as having **one additional sigil in every Arcane school** when checking prerequisites. After you resolve a Research action, you may immediately take a second Research action in the same turn, paying its full AP and resource costs.

**Limits:** The bonus sigils satisfy prerequisites only; they are not Discoveries and grant no printed effects. The second Research action is optional and usable once per round.
**Theme:** You research as though the empire never fell.

### 2) Scry the Council (Passive Ability)

**Effect:** At the start of the **High Council Phase**, you may look at the top card of the **agenda deck** (First Playable rule) and put it back.  
**Theme:** Your diviners hear the argument before it is spoken.

### 3) Blink Step (Active Ability)

**Requirement:** Seraphel must be part of the **moving group**.  
**Effect:** Once per round, when you take a **Move** action, you may spend **2 Mana**. If you do, treat **Mountains** and **Deserts** as costing **1 AP per hex** for that Move action (instead of 2 AP per hex).  
**Cost:** 2 Mana  
**Theme:** A step across ley lines is faster than any road.

## Faction Research (Arcanum Concord)

### Mana Nexus (Lord-Specific Discovery)

**Cost:** 4 Mana, 1 Gold (Research action)  
**Effect:** Your controlled **Mana-producing** hexes (Forests/Groves/unique Mana tiles) produce **+1 additional Mana**.  
**Theme:** You widen the conduits; the land itself becomes a focus.

### Spellweave Doctrine

**Cost:** 2 Mana, 2 Influence (Research action)  
**Effect:** Once per round, after you lobby on a High Council vote (spend any Influence during **Lobbying**), gain **1 Mana**.  
**Theme:** Politics is just another ritual circle.

## Faction Objectives

### Primary Objective (2 VP): “Ritual Ascendance”

End a round with **10 Mana** and control **at least 2 Forest/Grove** hexes.

### Secondary Objective (1 VP): “Calculated Violence”

In a single round, resolve **Press the Attack** at least **twice** across your attacks (this can be across multiple Attack actions), and win at least one of those battles.

---

## Faction Strategy

### Strengths

- **Research tempo:** Prerequisite flexibility and paired Research actions accelerate Arcane specialization.
- **Information edge:** Scry the Council improves negotiation and counterplay in politics.
- **Terrain flexibility:** Blink Step reduces the penalty of Mountains/Deserts for repositioning.

### Weaknesses

- **Mana hungry:** Blink Step and research both compete for the same resource.
- **Thin early economy:** Low starting Gold can delay defensive infrastructure.
- **Threat perception:** Efficient burst turns often make you the table’s priority target.

## Notes for Playtesting

- **Polymath:** Track the round of Seraphel's second and third Discoveries and whether paired Research turns create an unrecoverable lead.
- **Blink Step:** Verify it’s a meaningful mobility tool without trivializing terrain as a strategic feature.
- **Mana Nexus scaling:** Confirm +1 Mana on all Mana-hexes doesn’t outpace other economies too early.
