Simulated Playtest Report — 6 & 8 Players

Scaled-map fuzz campaign · chaos-bot stress test · July 2, 2026

TL;DR — The simulator now supports the full First Playable player range (3–8). In 300 new games at 6 and 8 players, the engine completed 97%+ with zero crashes. At higher counts the map grows (61 and 91 hexes) and games run longer, but the same structural pattern holds: the Imperial Seat still drives most VP, though objectives gain share as player count rises. Average winning margin remains ~7 VP — still a landslide in a 10-point race.

Scope note: Milestone 1 originally shipped with 3–4 player maps only. For this report the map generator was extended per First_Playable_Packet.md §3.1 (scaled neutral ring, larger disk). Lords, Strategy cards, Council, and Whispers are still absent — this is the same core engine with a bigger board.

3 · Observations

  • Scaling works mechanically. Larger maps, more players, longer games — and the engine stayed stable through ~150,000 combined actions with no crashes or state corruption.
  • Objectives gain share at 8 players. With more players pursuing the same five public objective types (some duplicated when the deck reshuffles), objectives overtook raw Seat income at 8p (44% vs 40%). Seat + streak still dominates combined, but the gap narrows vs 3–4 player runs (~73% Seat share in the earlier report).
  • Runaway wins persist. Average margin ~7.2 VP (6p) and ~7.0 VP (8p); mode margin 8 in both. More than half of non-winners finish at exactly 0 VP. One player rides the center to 10 while seven others watch.
  • Games get longer, not shorter. 8-player games averaged 14.3 rounds vs ~12 for 3–4 player configs — expected with more actors and a larger map, but still well within the 25-round cap (only 4 timeouts across 300 games).
  • Seat position still looks fair. Win counts per seat are noisy at ~18–35 wins each over 150 games; no clear first-player advantage, though 8p seat 3 and 7 look slightly elevated — worth watching with strategic bots.
  • Combat remains thin. Lord captures ~2.6–2.9% of VP — slightly higher than at 3–4p (~1%), likely because more players means more battles, but still negligible vs Seat income.

The headline balance concern — Seat dominance producing blowout wins — softens slightly as player count rises, but does not disappear. Objectives absorb some of the slack, yet nobody is contesting the center effectively under random play.

5 · What's next

  • Persona bots at 6–8p — the first test of whether skilled opponents can break Seat camping or whether the structure itself needs tuning.
  • Canon decisions on Seat VP rate, objective deck scaling at high player counts, and catch-up mechanics (Winds of Fortune is in the Packet but not yet in the engine).
  • Commit map scaling — the 5–8 player generator used here is tested but not yet merged; should land with the next sim milestone.
