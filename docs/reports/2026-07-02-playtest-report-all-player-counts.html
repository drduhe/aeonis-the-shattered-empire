3 · Key findings

  • Engine scales 3–8 without breaking. Map radius grows 3→4→5 (37/61/91 hexes), action counts scale linearly with players, and invariant checks passed across ~230,000 combined decisions with zero crashes.
  • Seat dominance softens but doesn't vanish. At 3–4 players the Seat economy (income + streak) supplies ~73–76% of all VP. By 7–8 players objectives overtake raw Seat income, but Seat + streak still accounts for ~53% — enough for one camper to win while seven others stall.
  • Blowout wins are count-invariant. Average winning margin stays 6.6–7.5 VP across all configurations. Roughly half of non-winners finish at exactly 0 VP regardless of player count.
  • 7 players is the rough edge. Highest timeout rate (6%) and longest average game (16 rounds). More actors on a large map without anyone pursuing VP efficiently produces longer stalemates before someone stumbles to 10.
  • 8 players is surprisingly stable. 98% completion, only 1 degenerate game — possibly because the larger board gives more random conquest paths to objectives.
  • Combat remains marginal. Lord capture: 0.6% (3p) to 3.6% (6p) of VP, never a primary scoring path under random play.
  • No seat bias detected. Win counts per seat are within noise at every player count (~10–33 wins per seat over 88–98 completed games).

4 · What this means for design

The First Playable ruleset appears structurally stable at every supported player count — the engine doesn't break, games finish, and pacing stays in a 6–25 round band. The open design question is the same at 3 players as at 8: the Imperial Seat is the default win condition under low-contest play, and objectives only partially compensate as player count rises (because more players share the same five objective types, increasing accidental completion).

Recommended next steps: persona bots at 4p and 8p as bracket tests; canon review of Seat VP rate and objective deck scaling; Winds of Fortune catch-up mechanic (in the Packet, not yet in the engine) as a counterweight to runaway Seat holders.

5 · Method & reproducibility

Each configuration: 100 games, chaos bots (uniform random legal moves), Milestone-1 engine (movement, combat, production, objectives, 10 VP threshold). Seeds are deterministic and logged — rerun any game with python3.11 -m aeonis_sim.runner.play --players N --seed S from the sim/ directory.
